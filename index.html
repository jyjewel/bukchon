The Story of Bukchon

Between Royal Palaces,
Walk the 'Gangnam' of Joseon

Bukchon was historically a prestigious neighborhood boasting great economic wealth and cultural refinement, in stark contrast to Namchon (South of Jonggak) where lower-ranking officials and poor scholars resided. Explore the paths at the heart of Gyeongbokgung and Changdeokgung palaces!

This course links seven historic sites across Bukchon, each tied to Korean cinema and modern history. Every card carries a short story and a photo mission — use them to build your own record of the walk.

01 / 서울특별시 종로구 북촌로7길 16

Baek In-je House

"Assassination (2015), Reborn Rich (2022)”

Built by Han Sang-ryong, a wealthy pro-Japanese businessman, to showcase immense wealth. It later became the home of Dr. Baek In-je, a genius surgeon who supported the independence movement. This is undeniably the grandest and most perfectly preserved Hanok mansion in the Bukchon area. As you enter, you must climb high stone stairs to pass the main gate. This design deliberately reflects the owner's intention to show off 'power and status distinct from others'.

The estate was constructed during the Japanese colonial period by Han Sang-ryong, an immensely wealthy pro-Japanese collaborator who operated the Hanseong Bank. He cleared 12 existing houses to build this single mega-mansion for high-society business gatherings. Unlike traditional Hanoks, this structure features expensive Japanese red glass and black pine, reflecting modern reformed styles. Its most unique feature is the long glazed veranda hallway connecting the quarters, which allowed guests to walk around without getting wet from the rain—a classic layout element borrowed from Japanese architecture.

Before liberation, the mansion was purchased by Dr. Baek In-je, a brilliant surgeon and secret financial supporter of the Provisional Government's independence movement. (He is the founder of the well-known Inje University Paik Hospital). Tragically, Dr. Baek was abducted to the North during the Korean War and passed away. His wife kept this tragedy quiet, spending over 60 years carefully maintaining the stone walls, gardens, and timber of this giant house, patiently waiting for his return and preserving its historic value for us today.

Just outside the estate stands 'Minju Kim', a modern fashion shop built with modified cement-frame Hanok architecture. It proves that Hanok continues to adapt and evolve through the layers of history.

Photo Spot Mission

"The Imposing Stone Entrance": Stand at the base of the towering entrance stone stairs. Look upward and snap a shot framing the steep, majestic ascent designed to show off societal power.

02 / 서울특별시 종로구 북촌로5길 48

Jeongdok Library (Honghyeon)

"A Hill of Scenery Preserved by Residents" Once the site of prestigious Kyunggi High School, this historic library garden features ancient trees, deep political roots, and beautiful seasonal foliage.

The sloping road leading up to the library's main gate is historically known as 'Honghyeon' (Red Hill). In the past, the local district office planned to level this steep slope to make way for easier automobile passage. However, local residents initiated a strong opposition campaign to protect the historic landscape of Bukchon. Their efforts successfully preserved the unique terraced entrance and steps that blend beautifully with the public restrooms today.

Jeongdok Library was once the campus of Kyunggi High School, formerly the most prestigious high school in Seoul. After the school relocated to Gangnam's Daechi-dong in the 1970s, the classic brick buildings were converted into a public library and public garden. Going deeper into history, this very site was the residential estate of Kim Ok-gyun, the modernization reformist who led the Gapsin Coup in 1884. It is also believed to be the spot where Gyeomjae Jeong Seon, the master painter of the late Joseon Dynasty, painted his masterpiece 'Inwangjesaekdo'.

Inside the lobby library, there is a lovely 'Nobel Literature Lounge' honoring author Han Kang's Nobel prize, and for lunch, try the Spam Cheese Kimbap from 'Haneul Kimbap' nearby on the lawns.

Photo Spot Mission

"Namsan Tower Framed View": Walk deep into the garden behind the library to locate the vintage white gothic stone archway. Frame Namsan Seoul Tower so it is positioned perfectly inside the archway silhouette.

03 / 서울특별시 종로구 송현동 34-2

Gamgodang-gil

"Empress's Family Estate and the Sparks of Female Rights" Named after Gamgodang, the childhood home of Empress Myeongseong. A historical path of human rights now meeting the wide, open lawn of Songhyeon Park.

This beautiful street is named 'Gamgodang-gil' because it was home to Gamgodang, the childhood residence where Empress Myeongseong (the last Queen of Joseon) lived and practiced her royal wedding rituals before marrying King Gojong.

In addition to its royal connections, this street is a significant cradle for women's human rights in Korea. Under the rigid hierarchy of the late Joseon Dynasty, noblewomen gathered here to issue historic declarations demanding a strict ban on the practice of concubinage. Their courageous collective voice marked a monumental step forward for institutional gender equality.

Recently, the high concrete walls that blocked off the site for decades were completely demolished to reveal the vast, green public lawn of 'Yeolrin Songhyeon' (Open Songhyeon Plaza). This transformation has turned the narrow stone-wall passage into a refreshing walking path harmonizing traditional Hanok aesthetics with a modern urban park.

Photo Spot Mission

"The Romantic Stone Wall Walk": Position yourself next to the long traditional clay-and-stone wall with the sweeping green lawn of Songhyeon Park stretching behind you.

04 / 서울특별시 종로구 윤보선길

Yun boseon-gil

"The Birthplace of Modern Democracy & Craftsmanship" A quiet, classy road parallel to Gamgodang-gil. Home to the massive, historic Hanok residence of President Yun Po-sun and beautiful artisanal galleries.

Running parallel to Gamgodang-gil, this elegant alleyway is named 'Yun boseon-gil' after the grand residence of Yun Po-sun, the 4th President of South Korea, which occupies a major portion of the hillside.

The Yun Po-sun Residence maintains the majestic scale of a late Joseon/early modern estate. During the harsh political struggles of modern Korean history, this house served as an invaluable sanctuary where democratic opposition leaders gathered to plan reforms. While the inner courtyard remains a private residence and is not fully open to the public, the gorgeous tile rooflines and historic brick pillars viewed from the street offer a spectacular glimpse into traditional elite architecture.

Along Yun boseon-gil, you will find the historic Andong Church, quiet ceramic studios, traditional bag designers, modern leather craft houses, and tiny independent art galleries. It provides a peaceful, artistic sanctuary for quiet contemplation away from the busy Seoul metropolitan center.

Photo Spot Mission

"Heritage & Modern Contrast": Frame the elegant dark wood eaves and tiled roofline of the President's Residence juxtaposed against the vintage red-brick pillars lining the narrow path.

05 / 서울 종로구 삼일대로 464 운현궁

Unhyeongung Palace

"The Epicenter of Late-Joseon Political Power" The home of Heungseon Daewongun, the absolute regent who executed isolationist reforms and expelled powerful clans during the turbulent late 19th century.

Unhyeongung is situated slightly south of the main Bukchon Hanok villages. If you feel tired during your walk, you can skip this location, but its historical scale is absolutely immense. This was the childhood residence (Jamjeo) of Emperor Gojong, who lived here before ascending the throne at the young age of 12.

In practice, it functioned as the political headquarters of his father, Heungseon Daewongun, who ruled Joseon as regent. Daewongun consolidated royal funds here and even constructed private palace-style gates connecting directly to the main palaces, emphasizing his supreme authority. This estate witnessed crucial decisions regarding modernization, isolationism, and national defense.

Every Friday evening (18:00~21:00), Unhyeongung offers a special night opening with gentle ambient lighting across its traditional courtyards. Directly behind it sits the white, European-style Yanggwan (Western-style Hall), which adds a beautiful architectural contrast.

Photo Spot Mission

"East & West Collision": Walk near the outer compound wall and align your camera to capture both the classic dark wood Hanok gate and the white, brick Neo-classical Yanggwan (Western Hall) standing tall behind it.

06 / 서울 종로구 계동

Gye-dong & Cultural Center

"The Heart of Local Daily Life in Bukchon" A cozy street containing vintage bathhouses transformed into modern showrooms, century-old oil presses, and the peaceful, welcoming courtyards of Bukchon Cultural Center.

Gye-dong Street is the alleyway that preserves the most authentic daily life of Bukchon's actual residents. Classic apothecary pillars, old school-district tax structures, a century-old sesame oil press, and retro black-and-white photo studios offer a nostalgic trip back in time.

The neighborhood's iconic public bathhouse, 'Jungangtang', has been creatively redesigned into a modern boutique showroom for a premium fragrance brand, preserving the old bath tubs and tiles in a trendy way. Located at the beginning of the alley, the 'Bukchon Cultural Center' and Baeryeom House are wonderful, free-of-charge Hanok complexes where you can sit and rest without any reservations.

Feel free to slip off your shoes and sit on the open Hanok wooden porch (Toetmaru) to enjoy the quiet, manicured garden. Every weekend, they host beautiful, free or highly affordable events like gayageum concerts or traditional bojagi wrapping workshops.

Photo Spot Mission

"The Retro Bathhouse Facade": Find the revitalized 'Jungang Bathhouse' storefront on Gye-dong. Capture the historic neon/blue retro bathhouse logo now acting as a chic modernized street icon.

07 / 서울특별시 종로구 창덕궁길 164

Choongang High School

"The Cradle of the March 1st Movement & K-Drama Capital" A historic nationalist academy founded during the late Joseon Dynasty. Famous for its majestic, grey gothic-style stone buildings and romantic campus views.

Choongang High School is a historic, elite private academy founded to foster national education and independence during the late Joseon era. In 1919, under severe Japanese oppression, the drafting of the historic March 1st Declaration of Independence was secretly plotted in these very buildings. It has produced many prominent modern literary figures, including poet Ki Hyeong-do and novelist Chae Man-sik.

Due to its breathtaking Western gothic architecture, dramatic central lawn, and grand stone towers, the school has been beloved as a primary filming location for decades. Because it is an active high school, public access is strictly restricted on weekdays to protect student learning. On weekends, partial access to the central lawn is occasionally permitted with the guard's permission.

Photo Spot Mission

"The Guardian Ginkgo Tree": Walk up the slight hill opposite the main school gate. Angle back toward the grand courtyard and capture the massive 500-year-old ginkgo tree frame alongside the gothic brick spire.
